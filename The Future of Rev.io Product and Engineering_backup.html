The Pod Structure: 3-Person Autonomous Units

Each pod operates as a Lean Agent Orchestra - three master orchestrators commanding comprehensive agent networks that span product, engineering, quality, and operations domains.

Pod Agent Distribution

Every Pod Member Gets Full Agent Access

  • All pod members receive comprehensive agent networks spanning product and engineering
  • Product-focused agents translate requirements and validate acceptance criteria
  • Engineering agents handle implementation, testing, and deployment
  • Quality assurance agents ensure standards across all deliverables
  • DevOps agents manage infrastructure and continuous deployment
  • No role silos - every orchestrator commands the full spectrum of capabilities

Key Innovation: By giving everyone access to all agent types, we eliminate handoffs and dependencies. Each pod member can take a feature from concept to production autonomously.

AI Engineering Lead

  • Primary Role: Chief orchestrator and technical architect
  • Designs the overall agent strategy for the team's domain
  • Orchestrates cross-functional agent networks spanning product and engineering
  • Final technical authority for production deployments
  • Mentors engineers in advanced orchestration techniques
  • Architects meta-agents that coordinate other agent networks
  • Ensures team delivers exponential value through sophisticated orchestration

AI Software Engineers (2)

  • Primary Identity: Builders of autonomous agent platforms
  • Architect and deploy self-organizing agent ecosystems
  • Design meta-orchestration systems where agents manage other agents
  • Build autonomous value chains from requirements to production
  • Create self-evolving agent networks that improve with each iteration
  • Master the art of prompt engineering at massive scale
  • Develop agent hierarchies that handle edge cases autonomously
  • Transform business problems into orchestrated agent solutions

Domain Knowledge Model

Domain Orchestration Model

Each pod becomes the master orchestrator for specific domains, deploying specialized agent networks that continuously expand their knowledge base. Domain expertise multiplies exponentially as agents learn, share, and spawn specialized sub-agents for increasingly complex tasks.

Domain Specialization Benefits

  • Deeper Expertise: Teams become increasingly proficient in their domains
  • Faster Development: Less context-switching between unrelated features
  • Higher Quality: Intimate knowledge of domain-specific requirements
  • Clear Ownership: Teams take pride in their areas of responsibility

New Development Focus

  • Isolated Innovation: New development areas (like SLAs) assigned to specific pods
  • Existing Enhancement: Features enhancing existing functionality (ticketing, quoting, workflows) assigned to domain expert pods
  • Balanced Workload: Each pod has at most one area of brand-new development
